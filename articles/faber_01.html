Alles Gute

by Faber

Have you ever seen a drummer who plays the trombone at the same time, using a broken plunger as a mute? If not, check out „Faber“ and his band!

The young artist from Switzerland comes from a very musical family – his father is the Italian singer/songwriter Pippa Pollina and his sister, Madlaina, released some great songs with her congenial partner as „Steiner & Madlaina“ as well.

His lyrics are surprisingly mature, often rude and always a bit obscure. In an interview about two years ago, he smiled and said, how beautiful it was, that people saw things in his lyrics, that he had never intended. Good lyrics knew more than its creator.

One of my favorite songs of Faber is „Alles Gute“ from his first album „Sei ein Faber im Wind“, released in 2017. It comes with a video, but there is also a cool live recording from the TV show „Inas Nacht“, which you might know from my first article about „Element of Crime“.

Line eight in the chorus seems to be the only line in this song, that needs some explaining. „am Boden sein“ (be on the ground), or as well „am Boden zerstört sein“ (be destroyed on the ground) is a German expression that could be translated to „be in a sorry state“, or a bit harder: „to hit rock bottom“.

Also note that he pronounces the word „bist“ in the chorus a bit weirdly. To be honest, I’m not sure if that’s due to his Italian-Swiss roots, or if he is just varying the pronunciation, to make it fit a little better. Anyway, normally, „bist“ wouldn’t rhyme on „gehörst“ 😉
