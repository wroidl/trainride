Berlin is pretty annoying nowadays. Or more precisely, the picture of Berlin as Europe's hipster paradise is annoying. Of course all these annoying people and places in fact exist, but they cannot cover up, that Berlin is also run-down, ugly, and for most parts not sexy at all. The band Isolation Berlin is representing that other melancholic, more honest and in that way much cooler and more likeable part of Berlin.

Isolation Berlin was founded in 2012 and they released their first single in 2015 (Annabelle). It was followed by their very successful first album in 2017 (Und aus den Wolken tropft die Zeit) and their second and latest album Vergifte Dich (2018).

As  for the sound... I think this is the third time I am comparing a singer with Rio Reiser and I promise, this will be the last time (at least until I featured an actual song by Rio Reiser here). But the rough voice of lead singer Tobi Bamborschke, his fragile but impressive performances and the rattling guitar sound is sometimes astoundingly close to Ton Steine Scherben, especially when an organ joins in. Anyway, it is an interesting mix of rock, punk and new wave, often claimed to be influenced by Joy Division and Velvet Underground.

Translated Song: Alles grau

Alles grau seems to be Isolation Berlin's most popular song (at least according to Spotify) and was released in 2016 on a sampler with some older EPs and singles (Berliner Schule/Protopop). If you prefer the lyrics in Italian: together with some other German singer-songwriter artists, Isolation Berlin brought the project Crucchi Gang to life and released an Italian version of Alles grau, called Tutto grigio.
