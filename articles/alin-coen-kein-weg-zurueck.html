It was 2010, when I first heard of Alin Coen - or more precisely, the Alin Coen Band - who played their song Festhalten in the German TV show "Inas Nacht". Her expressive and beautiful voice is extraordinary and the thoughtful lyrics tell stories beyond the usual cliches.

The band was founded in 2007 and about a year later, when they played on the singer-songwriter show "TV Noir", their recordings hit several hundred thousands views on YouTube.

After releasing two successful albums in 2010 (Wer bist du?), and 2013 (We’re Not the Ones We Thought We Were), Alin Coen made an interesting personal decision. She stated, that she didn't see much value in creating and performing music, while the world faces much bigger problems. Consequently, she returned to her studies and enrolled for a master's degree in "Land and Water Management" and worked as an intern at Greenpeace.

They still gave concerts and also released a live album in 2016 with the meaningful title Alles was ich hab (all I got), but unfortunately didn't release any new songs. Only recently, she felt the urge to create music again and it came as a big suprise to me, when they released the new album Nah (close) full of great songs in August 2020.

While the first album (Wer bist du?, 2010) was a mix of German and English lyrics and the 2013 album (We’re Not the Ones We Thought We Were) held almost only English songs, her latest release is entirely in German.

Translated Song: Kein Weg Zurück

It is always difficult to decide which of the songs I translate into English, but I take comfort in the thought, that I can just present all the other songs in following articles. So, as a first song to translate, I have chosen Kein Weg Zurück - one of the few German songs from their second album. It is a great example of her ability to tell a moving story within a short song and at the same time it accentuates her perfect and clear voice. Enjoy!

Line 23: it is not entirely clear, from what the idiom "Das Blatt wendet sich" is derived. It either refers to a hand of cards (= "Blatt") that changes the course of a game, or alternatively, it refers to a new page (= "Seite" or "Blatt") in a book that brings a twist to the story. In both cases, it could be translated to "The tide (or luck) has turned" or "The boot is on the other foot".
