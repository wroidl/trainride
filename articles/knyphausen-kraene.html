I remember very well, that I was really looking forward to the release of Gisbert's second album "Hurra! Hurra! So nicht." in 2010. And that it then took me quite a while to get really into it, since it is far deeper and darker than his successful debut album (see Sommertag from that album). Very often, the second release is not able to hold a candle to the first success, simply because of the hastiness to use the momentum and build up on the current success. But it seems that Gisbert was able to handle this pressure and he created a couple of great songs.

One of them is "Kräne" ("Cranes"). The lyrics draw some nice images of the industrial harbour of Hamburg, where the cranes come to life as tremendous animals with neon light eyes, that drop containers into the bellys of old and hungry freighters.

By the way, the following video is from "TV noir". They call themselves "the livingroom of songwriters" and host a bunch of great concerts. It's definitely worth to browse through their recordings!

In line 27, "Schlag deine krummen Flügel um mich" literally means "Hit your crooked wings around me", but "to hit around something" in this context means to "to wrap around something". This is also used quite often for example to say that something is wrapped in paper. Here you would say: "es ist in Papier eingeschlagen".
