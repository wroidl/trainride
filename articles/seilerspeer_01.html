Ham kummst

by Seiler und Speer

To understand German lyrics might be quite difficult, if you are not a native speaker and not too familiar with German culture. But when it comes to dialects, even many Germans  have to surrender. In this article, I tried to translate an Austrian song first to "normal" German and than into English. I wasn't really surprised, that I had to consult dictionaries more offen to get the German right, than to find a proper translation into English.

"Seiler und Speer" is a duo from Vienna. Christopher Seiler (producer and director) and Bernhard Speer (comedian) started the band at first as a little side project, but after their first album "Ham kummst" was surprisingly successful, they became more serious about their musical ambitions and have released three albums until now.

The music is simple and considering their backgrounds, the main focus is the storytelling rather than the music itself. The lyrics represent the typical and even for Germans hard to understand dialect from Vienna. The song "Ham kummst", which gave the title to their first album, is their most famous piece (although I'm not a big fan of the pretty clownish video. Which is ironic, given that Seiler is a director himself...).

55) Pücha (or Pücher/ Pülcher) is a historical dialect from Vienna, that 99% of Germans won't understand as well. It derives from the word pilgrim but has a bad meaning. Since back in the middle ages, there were supposedly many fake pilgrims in Vienna who claimed food and shelter, it is a swearword today to name someone, who claims something to which he has no right.
